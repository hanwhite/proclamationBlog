The Role of Mothers to be Nurturers
Motherhood is continuously degraded and undervalued by the world. This degrading is the work of the adversary. Many involved in the feminist movement believe that motherhood makes women vulnerable and should be avoided, because it means women are dependent on a man for financial reasons. However, is that not the point of marriage? Are the men not dependent on the women to care for their children with every ounce of love and selflessness that they have, teaching them to be good people and nurturing them with all of their heart? Despite the determination the adversary has to degrade motherhood as much as possible, motherhood will never be less than the most important, significant, and divine role a woman can ever have.


The responsibilities mothers have are central in preserving the lives of their children and fostering their children’s growth and development, mentally and physically. Effective mothers are sensitive to their child’s needs, and are responsive to them. Mothers have a profound amount of influence on the children they raise. A secure attachment between mother and child results in a child that feels that they deserve to be loved, and they have an easier time learning to understand and empathize with others. Individual adaptation is required for unique situations, and it is important to remember the words of Sheri Dew, who said, “Every one of us can show by word and by deed that the work of women in the Lord’s kingdom is magnificent and holy. I repeat: We are all mothers in Israel, and our calling is to love and help lead the rising generation through the dangerous streets of mortality.”

It is just as essential for mothers to care for themselves as it is for them to care for their children. Elder M. Russell Ballard explains, “Find some times for yourself to cultivate your gifts and interests. Pick one or two things that you would like to learn or do that will enrich you life, and make time for them. Water cannot be drawn from an empty well, and if you are not setting aside a little time for what replenishes you, you will have less and less to give to others, even to your children.” Motherhood is a demanding calling that is not always filled with perfect, joyful moments. By making time for themselves, mothers give themselves the ability to fill their “well”, so they may continue to give to others.
